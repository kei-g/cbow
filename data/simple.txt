How did planets form? Some planets formed close to the sun. Some planets formed far from the sun. The sun is very hot. Planets close to the sun needed to be made of stuff that can handle heat. Rock and metal can handle heat. So, planets close to the sun, like Earth, are made of rock and metal. These are small planets.

Other planets formed far from the sun. It is cold far from the sun. This cold area is past the cold line. Big gas planets like Jupiter and Saturn formed far from the sun. They formed in the cold area. They are made of gas stuff that becomes solid in the cold. Jupiter is a big gas planet. Saturn is a big gas planet. They formed far from the sun where it is cold.

But scientists saw something strange in other star systems. They saw big gas planets very close to their stars. These planets are like Jupiter, but they are close to their star, where it is hot. Scientists called these planets "hot Jupiters". Hot Jupiters are big gas planets close to their star. This was a puzzle. Why are big gas planets found close to their star? We thought big gas planets formed far from the star where it is cold.

To understand hot Jupiters, scientists suggested a new idea. The idea is that planets can move. Planets do not stay where they first formed. This idea is called planets moving. Scientists tested this idea using Jupiter. They made a model called the "grand tack" idea.

In the grand tack idea, Jupiter formed far from the sun. Jupiter formed past the cold line, like we thought. Jupiter is a big gas planet, and it formed far out. But then, Jupiter started moving. Jupiter moved close to the sun. It moved into the area where planets are made of rock and metal. Jupiter moved like the hot Jupiters we see in other star systems.

But Jupiter did not stay close to the sun. Why? Because of Saturn. Saturn is another big gas planet in our star system. Saturn formed far from the sun too. The pull from Saturn, the other big gas planet, pulled Jupiter. Saturn's pull made Jupiter move back out. Jupiter moved away from the sun again. Jupiter moved back to the area far from the sun, where it is now. So Jupiter took a trip: formed far out, moved close in, then moved far out again. This trip is the grand tack idea.

This grand tack idea helps explain hot Jupiters. It shows that big gas planets can move close to their star. Even if Jupiter moved back out, other big gas planets in other star systems might stay close to their star. So the grand tack idea explains hot Jupiters.

The grand tack idea also explains other strange things in our star system. For example, Mars is small. Computer models showed Mars should be bigger. Why is Mars small? The grand tack idea gives an answer. When Jupiter moved close to the sun, its strong pull pushed away lots of stuff. Jupiter pushed away the stuff near the sun. This was the stuff that Mars could have used to become bigger. Because Jupiter pushed the stuff away during its trip, Mars stayed small. So Jupiter's trip explains why Mars is small.

Also, think about Earth. Earth is a rocky planet close to the sun. Life can live on Earth. If Jupiter did not make its trip, things might be different. If Jupiter stayed far out, more gas stuff might have stayed close to the sun. Then, planets close to the sun, like Earth, might have become big gas planets. If Earth was a big gas planet, life could not live here. Many other star systems have big planets close to their star. These are not rocky planets like Earth. Our star system seems special because our planets close to the sun are rocky.

The grand tack idea suggests why our star system is special. Jupiter's trip helped make it this way. Jupiter moved close to the sun and pushed away gas stuff. This allowed small, rocky planets like Earth to form close to the sun. This allowed a place for life. Most other star systems we see have big gas planets close to their star. They don't have rocky planets like Earth close in. So our star system is maybe very special.

If the grand tack idea is right, Jupiter's trip was very important. Jupiter moving close to the sun and then moving back out shaped our star system. It helped make Mars small. It helped make Earth a rocky planet where life can live. We should thank Jupiter for its long trip through our star system. Jupiter's movement made our home possible. The grand tack idea shows how the movement of big gas planets like Jupiter can change a whole star system.
